Student Voices

In their own words

Direct quotes — selected for themes that were highly recurring or uniquely significant, even if uncommon.

Wellbeing — personal
"You could invite more people to the wellbeing room who are having a bad day — I have been invited to the room before but only 2 times in the 2 years I have been here."
Signals a proactive outreach gap, not a resource gap
Teacher conduct
"Teachers should stop screaming at kids and using fear to get kids to do what they want... teachers here that scream and harshly discipline children have a profoundly negative impact on students' wellbeing."
Consistent across multiple independent responses
Assessment anxiety
"Separate assessments by a week — it's hard to remember 7 subjects' info all in one week... spreading them out is a lot more calming and helps with potential anxiety."
Specific, actionable, low-cost scheduling change
Mental health access
"Put a sign up near the library telling students where the mental health room is."
A tiny intervention that could have high impact — students don't know it exists
Social inclusion
"Make sure that no one gets left out or feels like they're not good enough" and "create a more accepting environment."
Aligns with the school's stated values of trust, care and resilience
Outdoor safety
"Add grass instead of quad because falling on the quad hurts a lot... if we open the oval across the road at lunch we can all play and be outside."
Safety concern + practical use of Balgowlah oval nearby
Equal treatment
"Equal student rights and no favourability" and "treating people how we want to be treated."
Fairness perception — consistency in staff behaviour matters to students
Curriculum relevance
"Subjects that help our everyday lives" and "better electives" and "allow 3 electives in Year 9 and 10."
Students want curriculum relevance alongside academic rigour

Student Council Recommendations

What we're asking for

Prioritised by ease of implementation and student impact — realistic, low-cost, and directly supported by survey data. Consistent with the school's values of aspiration, care, and resilience.

Low effort
Sign for the mental health room
Students don't know the wellbeing room exists. A single sign near the library is potentially the highest-ROI action in this entire report.
Low effort
Ball borrowing system from PE
PE equipment already exists. A simple sign-out system at lunch gives students something active to do outdoors immediately.
Low effort
Unlock bathrooms at recess & lunch
The most repeated basic access request in the survey. A matter of dignity and daily comfort for every student.
Low effort
Proactive wellbeing room outreach
One student reported only two invitations in two years. The infrastructure exists — the gap is proactive reach-out.
Medium effort
Stagger assessment schedules
A scheduling change, not a resource change. Students explicitly linked clustering of assessments to anxiety and reduced performance.
Medium effort
Review canteen pricing
The #1 theme in the survey with ~110 mentions. The school's own canteen page is a natural starting point for a pricing review.
